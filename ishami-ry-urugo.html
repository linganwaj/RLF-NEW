Restoring Dignity to Couples Facing Infertility

“Ishami ry’Urugo” program contributes to supporting Rwandan couples facing infertility by offering medical, emotional, and social care.

The program helps reduce stigma and increase access to fertility screening, counseling, and Assisted Reproductive Technologies (ART). In partnership with Legacy ARC and other health centers, the program builds a safe space for families to heal and find hope.

Key Objectives

  • Providing affordable access to advanced fertility assessments and ART support.
  • Reducing the cultural stigma associated with infertility through community education.
  • Offering emotional resilience training and psychological counseling.

Our Impact So Far

4,000+ Community members sensitized on infertility stigma.
600+ Individuals provided with subsidized screenings.
1,200+ Therapy sessions conducted by trained counselors.
16 Couples sponsored for full ART treatment over 2 years.
Methodology

How We Work

Support Groups

Creating peer-to-peer networks where families can share experiences and support each other's journeys.

Medical Partnership

Leveraging Legacy ARC's world-class facilities to provide state-of-the-art reproductive care.

Community Outreach

Active sensitization campaigns to change the narrative around fertility in local communities.

Strategic Roadmap

Our goal for 2025-2026 is to expand our reach to more underserved regions in Rwanda, ensuring that distance is not a barrier to family dreams.

2025 Q1
Regional Expansion

Establishing satellite counseling centers in the Eastern and Western Provinces.

2025 Q3
Advanced ART Lab

Upgrading our partner facilities with the latest in embryology technology.

2026
National Advocacy

Partnering with policymakers to integrate fertility care into national health schemes.
