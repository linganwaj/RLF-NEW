Early Detection for a Healthier Future

Named ‘Amagara N’Igicumbi’ meaning ‘Health is the Foundation’ in Kinyarwanda, the initiative reflects the belief that strong communities are built on accessible healthcare, prevention, and early intervention.

“Amagara N’Igicumbi” is a community-based program that aims to reduce the burden of non-communicable diseases (NCDs) through early detection, health education, and follow-up care.

The program delivers mobile health screening camps, providing free screening services for diabetes, hypertension, and cancer. It combines patient outreach with referral systems, public awareness, and local health worker training to promote healthy lifestyles and timely care.

Our Focus Areas

  • Mobile screening camps for early detection of diabetes and hypertension.
  • Cancer awareness and early diagnostic referrals.
  • Public health education on nutrition and lifestyle changes.

Program Reach

120,000+ People reached through community education campaigns.
350+ Health workers and volunteers trained across 60+ communities.
$750k+ Mobilized in funding and medical equipment for outreach.

Real World Impact

Pilot Project Success

June Pilot Program

Our initial pilot project far surpassed expectations, demonstrating the high level of community eagerness for health services.

  • Initial Target: 500 People
  • Actual Turnout: 1,600+ People (3x Target)
  • Location: Local Health Facility

The success was driven by a synergy with existing services and high community awareness of the importance of preventive care.

“Know Your Numbers” Campaign

Our flagship awareness initiative designed to bring vital health data directly to the people.

Proactive Screening

Identifying high-risk individuals early to encourage lifestyle changes before severe outcomes occur.

Long-term Management

Reducing mortality and morbidity through consistent follow-up care for chronic conditions.

The “Healthy Villages” Model

A strategic focus on intensive, deep-impact intervention to establish replicable models for self-sustaining health improvements within select communities.

  • Building local capacity for health promotion
  • Establishing community health champions
  • Promoting collective responsibility
